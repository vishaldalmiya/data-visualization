I have used interactive slideshow as the medium for the narrative visualization for dataset related to "Honey Production in US". Next and Prev buttons are used to navigate the slide show with options to drill down using tooltip which displays information about the parameters when the user hover over the data point of interest.

  • Scenes: Same template has been used across all scenes (e.g. similar formatting, fonts, svg size, etc). There are namely 4 scenes used for the interactive slide show:
    • Overview: This scene provides an overview of what the dataset is, where it is obtained from and what questions will be answered as part of this narrative visualization.
    • Total production: This scene represents the cumulative honey production per state from year 1998-2012. To show the states with the highest and lowest honey production from 1998-2012, a choropleth map has been used which provides an overview as to the relative honey production among the different states in US. The saturation has been used to reflect the relative honey production among states (cumulative of 1998-2012) & only the states with highest and lowest total production have been labeled with the state name and total production to draw users attention to the same. Details for the other states will show up in tooltip
    • Total production trend: This scene shows the total honey production trend across states for years 1998-2012. The X axis represents the year (independent variable) and the Y axis represents the total production of honey (dependent variable) per year in lbs. Line chart with different colors represent the total production per state. A line chart was chosen here since year is a continuous variable. Different colors were added to different state since its a nominal data. A line chart and not stacked line chart was used to understand the trend better per state which helps in the inference section. The years shown were in the range of 1998-2012 during which only the data was available. Legend was added to identify the color code states. Only the top 7 & bottom 2 states based on total production are chosen else the charts were very crowded
    • Price per lb trend: This scene shows the price per lb trend across states for years 1998-2012. The X axis represents the year (independent variable) and the Y axis represents the price per lb (dependent variable) of honey per year in lbs. Line chart with different colors represent the price per lb per state. A line chart was chosen here since year is a continuous variable. Different colors were added to different state since its a nominal data. The years shown were in the range of 1998-2012 during which only the data was available. Legend was added to identify the color code states. Only the top 7 & bottom 2 states based on total production are chosen else the charts were very crowded
  • Annotations: Following are the annotations that were added to the relevant slides:
    • Total production: The main data to highlight here were the states with highest and lowest total production of honey for which the corresponding states have been marked with their state name and the total production.
    • Total production trend: An annotation was added to the year 2006. This was to help the reader to demarcate the trend in total production of honey before and after the year 2006 (the year of "Colony Collapse Disorder").
    • Price per lb trend: When the scene transitioned from to Total production trend to Price per lb trend, the previous annotation was cleared and a new one was added for the year 2003 to emphasize to the readers that for states OK & SC the price per lb increased drastically by 2003, the same year they ceased production as well. This was done to show that price could have played a role in the reduction in honey production post 2006.
  • Parameters:
    • Total production: The main parameters in this scene are the states, their cummulative total honey production and the year range (1998-2012) over which the cummulation was done .
    • Total production trend: The main parameters here are the state, total production per year and the year range (1998-201).
    • Price per lb trend: The main parameters here are the state, price per lb per year and the year range (1998-201).
  • Triggers: The main triggers for the user are the Next and Previous buttons on every scene which enabled the user to navigate through slides. These buttons rendered new scenes with different parameters. Examples of the same:
    • When the user navigates from the scene Total production to Total production trend, using the Next button, the maps changes to a line chart and instead of displaying the total production cummulative for all years it shows total production per year.
    • When the user navigates from the scene Total production trend to Price per lb trend, using the Next button, the X axis remains year but Y axis changes from total production per year per state to price per lb per year per state.
    • Vice-versa happens for the above scenes when user clicks on Previous button.
